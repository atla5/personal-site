The Adapter Pattern

Aidan Sawyer

The adapter pattern is meant to unify two disparate systems in a pure input output method by means of a common interface. Adapters are useful in quickly communicating between two systems, protocols, or file types that share a lot of information or functionality.

Adapters are limited in that they are devoted to the specific task of translation and not designed for the addition of any functionality. When daisy-chained or over-used, they may present a hazard to readability and/or processing speed. They should only be used in the event that you are trying to bridge two external formats or data streams.

It is used very commonly in converting file/data types, for example: XML and JSON, or .docx to .rtf. Another very common use is in utilizing APIs.
