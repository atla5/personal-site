About Me

My name is Aidan Sawyer. I'm a Web Developer with a great appreciation for simplicity, interoperability, and separation of concerns.

My goal and purpose on github - and in my career more generally - is to create and contribute to projects that serve to increase the efficiency, depth, and reach of scholarship and knowledge production that power human achievement (and enjoyment) across time and in perpetuity.

This has lead me to positions in and around academic research and libraries, fueled my interest and contributions to open source and open scholarship, and it's what's led me to create lib-re oa-sys.

How I Work

I seek, wherever possible (and practical), to push the boundaries of what can be done with the web platform itself, without defaulting to the huge framework-of-the-day, some elaborate and complicated build system, a variety of large extraneous libraries, and even - it's true - without vanilla javascript or CSS.

There are many guiding design principles in support of why I do this - which I (have/will) expand upon in my blog - but the most directly applicable term I've found so far is the principle of least power. It basically says, don't use a fancy, new sledge-hammer when you can use a trusty, regular old handheld one.

I follow this principle so doggedly for a number of reasons:

  • I want to learn the web itself, not a fashionable (but fleeting) abstraction on top of it
  • I want the capabilities of the web platform to grow and compound over time
  • I want my work to be more performant, accessible, and interoperable
  • I want the skills that I learn to remain useful over my entire career
  • I want to ensure (for myself and for others) that my work lasts (doesn't break)
  • I want my want the projects I work on to be easy to maintain, update, and predict
  • I find it challenging and engaging to test the capacities and find new solutions
